Afrida Rahman

Research Assistant · AI & Medical Imaging

Kundu Lab, Dept. of Radiology & Medical Imaging, University of Virginia, USA

Hi, I am a machine learning researcher working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and human health. My conviction is a simple one: that learning systems can read the signatures of disease — in a protein sequence, in a tissue, in a scan, with a precision the unaided eye cannot reach, and this precision can reshape how disease is diagnosed and treated.

At the University of Virginia, I work under the supervision of Dr. Bijoy Kundu, designing multimodal deep learning systems that integrate parametric FDG-PET with structural MRI to distinguish true tumor progression from treatment-induced necrosis in glioblastoma, a distinction that directly governs clinical decision-making in post-treatment neuro-oncology. A part of this work has been published in the Journal of Nuclear Medicine.

My research foundations were built over four years at the Machine Learning Research Lab, Bangladesh, where I developed sequence-based predictors for protein post-translational modification (PTM) site identification, a range of work published across Nature Scientific Reports, PLOS ONE, Gene, and IEEE/ACM Transactions on Computational Biology and Bioinformatics.

In a distinct line of applied work, I served as an AI Engineer at MyMedicalHUB, FL, USA where I brought deep learning to clinical-scale production — engineering real-time pose estimation and body-part segmentation to track over 600 musculoskeletal rehabilitation exercises on Android. Across these domains, my aim remains constant: to build models that are not only accurate, but trustworthy enough to inform real clinical decisions.
